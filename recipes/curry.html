Japanese Curry

Description

Introduced in the late 1800's to Japan by the British Navy, curry was quickly adopted and adapted by the Japanese. By 1905 Japanese curry powder was being produced commercially and by 1920 the predecessors of S&B and House began selling curry powder. Curry has gained popularity continuously since then in Japan, with many prefectures as well as military units having their own unique recipes.

Japanese curry is noticeably more muted and subtle than standard Indian curry. It is also much sweeter in many cases due to the Japanese integrating sweet fruits like apples into their recipes. Overall the flavor of Japanese curry is very akin to a well spiced stew, which goes well when paired with rice to absorb the gravy.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb meat (beef, chicken, pork), chopped
  • 2 medium onions, minced
  • ½ carrot, chopped (~3.5 oz)
  • 1 medium potato, chopped (~5 oz)
  • 2¼ cups dashi stock (or water)
  • 1 pack of curry roux
  • oil for pan

Steps

  1. Add meat to a hot, oiled pan on medium-high heat. Once fat starts to render add your vegetables.
  2. Stir fry meat and vegetables for about five minutes.
  3. Add Dashi stock (or water) and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover and simmer until tender. ~15 minutes
  4. Remove hear and break up curry roux. Add to pan and stir until roux is melted.
  5. Return to heat and simmer another 5 minutes, stirring constantly.
  6. Serve over rice or noodles.

Optional: Marinate the meat for 24 hours with the karaage marinade recipe for extra juicy meat. Pat dry before adding marinated meat to pan. You can also dredge the marinated meat im flour or cornstarch for extra flavor.
