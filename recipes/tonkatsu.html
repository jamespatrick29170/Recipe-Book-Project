Tonkatsu

Description

In Japan, tonkatsu is considered one of "The Three Great Yoshoku" alongside curry rice and and korokke (similar to a croquette). Much like other Yoshoku dishes, tonkatsu has it's roots in European cuisine and was developed during the Meiji era. Dredged in light flour, then egg, then panko, tonkatsu manages to retain incredible juiciness while also having a delightful crunch. Most commonly it's thin sliced and accompanied by rice or cabbage as well as katsu sauce. Katsu sauce is a thick worcestershire like sauce comparable to BBQ sauce. Often times Japanese curry and tonkatsu are combined to create Katsu curry.
