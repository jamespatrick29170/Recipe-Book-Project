Tonkatsu

Description

In Japan, tonkatsu is considered one of "The Three Great Yoshoku" alongside curry rice and and korokke (similar to a croquette). Much like other Yoshoku dishes, tonkatsu has it's roots in European cuisine and was developed during the Meiji era. Unlike curry which was derived from British recipes, Tonkatsu finds it's origin in côtelette de veau a french dish of veal cutlet, breaded and panfried in butter.

Dredged in light flour, then egg, then panko, tonkatsu manages to retain incredible juiciness while also having a delightful crunch. Most commonly it's thin sliced and accompanied by rice or cabbage as well as katsu sauce. Katsu sauce is a thick worcestershire like sauce comparable to BBQ sauce. Often times Japanese curry and tonkatsu are combined to create Katsu curry.

Ingredients

Tonkatsu

  • 4 boneless pork cuts, 4-6 oz/piece
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1½ cups Panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup AP flour
  • 3 large eggs beaten
  • Oil for frying

Katsu Sauce

  • ½ cup ketchup
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1½ tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Steps

Tonkatsu

  1. One at a time pound out your pork chops to ¼ inch thickness.
  2. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Prepare a dredging station in this order: flour, egg wash, panko.
  4. Fill a pan with ~⅓ inch of oil and bring to 350° F.
  5. Flour, dredge and bread your cutlets. Add to pan in two batches.
  6. Let brown for about a 1.5 minutes, then flip. Repeat this 3 more times. Cook ~6 minutes total.
  7. Transfer to wire rack or plate with paper towels to dry and season with salt.
  8. Slice thinly and serve with rice and katsu sauce.

Katsu Sauce

  1. Gather Ingredients
  2. Combine in a medium bowl
  3. Stir together until smooth and sugar dissolves
  4. Let sit in fridge at least 30 minutes
  5. Serve and enjoy
