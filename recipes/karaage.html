Chicken Karaage

Description

Chicken karaage is first referenced in the late 17th century. The dish was popularized the 1930's as "Chinese-style" food. Karaage most typically refers to chicken, which has been diced into small chunks, marinated and then dredged in cornstarch/flour before frying. Chicken karaage is so popular nowadays that it's sold in conbinis like 7-Eleven and Lawson.

Karaage when done properly should come out incredibly crispy on the outside and still exceptionally juicy on the inside. Marinated for up to 48 hours in a combination of soy sauce, mirin, worcestershire and various spices it's also extremely flavorful. You can change the meat used or play with the marinade levels to your hearts content.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced or grated
  • 1 lb chicken thigh, boneless, skinless, cubed
  • cornstarch

Steps

  1. Mix together all liquids, garlic and ginger. Marinate cubed chicken in this mixture for 24-48 hours. Minimum of 30 minutes.
  2. Remove chicken from marinade and dredge in cornstarch making sure distribute evenly and fully.
  3. Get 1½-2 inches of oil in a cast iron pan. Place on heat and get the oil up to ~350° F.
  4. Without overcrowding the pan add several pieces of chicken at a time. Frying for ~3 minutes, or until browned.
  5. As each batch finishes cooking rest them on a plate with paper towels to drain and finish.
  6. Serve while hot with rice or shredded cabbage.
